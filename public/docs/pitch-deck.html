01 / The Problem
The agency model is a Time & Materials relic
Every agency invoice buys hours — not outcomes. The billable hour is the bug in a $35B industry that hasn't fundamentally changed since 1999.
💸
The Agency Tax is structural
A minimum retainer of $1,500–$5,000/month before a single ad runs — then 10–15% of every dollar you spend on top. Agencies bill for inputs. They are structurally incentivised to grow your spend, not your returns. The model optimises for invoice size.
$18,000+
Annual minimum commitment — before ad spend, before results
⏰
Human latency has a dollar cost
A campaign burning budget on irrelevant terms at 2am Saturday gets reviewed Monday morning. Human attention operates in weekly cycles, not real-time. A $2,000/month campaign running unmonitored over a single weekend loses approximately $200 in pure wasted spend — before the agency opens their laptop. That's before you pay their retainer.
72hrs
Average lag before a human reviews an underperforming campaign — ~$200 wasted per unmonitored weekend per $2K/mo account
📊
Expertise doesn't scale past 12 accounts
One senior SEM strategist can actively manage 8–12 accounts with genuine attention. That is the hard ceiling of the agency model. Every new client added degrades the service delivered to the rest. In the agency world, scale equals degradation.
8–12
Maximum accounts one human can actively manage well — the ceiling that never moves
🔒
Tools still require humans to operate them
Optmyzr, Skai, and SA360 surface data — they don't make decisions. SMBs still need to hire an expert to interpret them. The 'SEM tool layer' hasn't eliminated the agency relationship. It's added a second cost on top of the first.
$150M
WordStream exit (2018) — the last major attempt. Still required experts to operate it.

Submit your URL and Gemini Vision AI reads your website the way a senior brand strategist would: extracting brand identity, tone of voice, products, and competitive positioning in minutes. No brief. No onboarding call. No account manager. The Spectra agents then build your campaigns and manage them continuously — 24 hours a day, every day. You move from in-the-loop (doing the work) to on-the-loop (directing the strategy).
